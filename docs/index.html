VerifyFill

Purpose

VerifyFill exists so you can finish signing in to websites without constantly switching between Gmail and the site you are on. When a service sends a verification code or a confirmation link to your email, this application finds it and helps you use it on the page where you are trying to log in or create an account.

How it works

VerifyFill is a Chrome extension. After you install it and sign in with Google:

  • You open a website that asks for a verification code or tells you to check your email.
  • VerifyFill checks your recent Gmail messages for a matching code or link.
  • If one is found, it shows a prompt on the page you are using.
  • You choose to fill the code into the form or open the confirmation link.

Data we access

VerifyFill accesses the following Google user data:

  • Your Google account email address
  • Recent messages in your Gmail inbox

It requests these OAuth scopes:

  • https://www.googleapis.com/auth/userinfo.email
  • https://www.googleapis.com/auth/gmail.readonly

How we use your data

Your email address is used only to show which Google account is connected in the extension.

Gmail messages are read only to search for verification codes and email confirmation links. Message content is processed on your device inside the Chrome extension. VerifyFill does not send your email to a server, does not modify or delete messages, and does not use your data for advertising or profiling.

Why we need this data

VerifyFill cannot find verification codes or confirmation links without read access to Gmail, because that is where websites send them. Read-only Gmail access is required to detect those messages and offer to autofill a code or open a link on the site you are using. Your email address is required so you can sign in with Google and so the extension can show which account is connected. No other Google data is accessed.

You can sign out in the extension or revoke access at any time in your Google Account permissions.
