VerifyFill

Chrome Extension

VerifyFill is developed and owned by Kristijonas.

Purpose

VerifyFill exists so you can finish signing in to websites without constantly switching between Gmail and the site you are on. When a service sends a verification code or a confirmation link to your email, this application finds it and helps you use it on the page where you are trying to log in or create an account.

Gmail access (gmail.readonly)

VerifyFill requests read-only access to Gmail through the OAuth scope https://www.googleapis.com/auth/gmail.readonly. This permission is used only to search your inbox for verification codes and email confirmation links. VerifyFill cannot send, delete, or modify your email.

Specifically, the extension:

  • Reads up to the 20 most recent inbox messages received in the last 15 minutes
  • Reads each message’s subject line and body text
  • Looks only for verification codes and sign-in or confirmation links
  • Ignores messages that do not appear related to verification

How it works

VerifyFill is a Chrome extension. After you install it and sign in with Google:

  • You open a website that asks for a verification code or tells you to check your email.
  • VerifyFill searches your inbox using the Gmail access described above.
  • If a matching code or link is found, it shows a prompt on the page you are using.
  • You choose to fill the code into the form or open the confirmation link.

Data we access

VerifyFill accesses the following Google user data:

  • Google account email address — via https://www.googleapis.com/auth/userinfo.email
  • Gmail inbox messages — via https://www.googleapis.com/auth/gmail.readonly: up to 20 inbox messages from the last 15 minutes; subject and body are read to detect verification codes and confirmation links only

How we use your data

Your email address is used only to show which Google account is connected in the extension.

Gmail message subject lines and bodies are read only to search for verification codes and email confirmation links. That content is processed on your device inside the Chrome extension. VerifyFill does not send your email to a server, does not modify or delete messages, and does not use your data for advertising or profiling.

Why we need this data

VerifyFill cannot find verification codes or confirmation links without read access to Gmail, because that is where websites send them. The gmail.readonly scope is required to read inbox message subjects and bodies from the last 15 minutes and detect codes or links to offer on the site you are using. Your email address is required so you can sign in with Google and so the extension can show which account is connected. No other Google data is accessed.

You can sign out in the extension or revoke access at any time in your Google Account permissions.
