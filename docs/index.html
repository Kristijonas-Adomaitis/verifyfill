Chrome Extension

Verification codes from Gmail, filled instantly

VerifyFill reads verification codes and confirmation links from your Gmail inbox and offers to autofill them on the website you are signing in to — no more tab switching.

Developed and owned by Kristijonas.

Download for Chrome Privacy Policy

Purpose

Finish signing in without leaving the page

VerifyFill exists so you can complete website sign-in, account creation, and two-step verification without constantly switching between Gmail and the site you are on. When a service sends a verification code or confirmation link to your email, this application finds it and helps you use it on the page where you are trying to log in or create an account.

How it works

Four steps to autofill

Install VerifyFill, sign in with Google, and let it handle the rest.

1

Install & sign in

Load the extension in Chrome and connect your Gmail account via Google Sign-In.

2

Visit a sign-in page

Open a site that asks for a verification code or tells you to check your email.

3

VerifyFill watches Gmail

The extension searches your recent inbox for a matching code or confirmation link.

4

One click to fill

A prompt appears on the page — click to autofill the code or open the link.

Functionality

Everything VerifyFill does

VerifyFill does not send email, delete email, read your full mailbox, or operate any server that receives your inbox content.

Google Sign-In

Connect your Gmail account through the extension popup.

Code detection

Finds numeric and alphanumeric OTP codes in recent inbox messages.

Link detection

Finds email confirmation and sign-in links in recent messages.

Autofill prompt

Shows an on-page prompt near your input when a match is found.

One-click fill

Inserts codes into focused fields, including multi-box OTP forms.

One-click open

Opens detected verification links when you choose.

Auto watch

Starts watching when you focus a code field or land on a verify-email page.

Background checks

Periodically checks Gmail while signed in for new codes and links.

Check now

Manual inbox scan from the extension popup at any time.

Used-item memory

Does not re-prompt for codes, links, or messages you already used.

Sign out

Disconnect Google and clear stored verification history.

Transparency

Why VerifyFill requests your data

VerifyFill only works if it can know which Google account you connected, read recent Gmail messages that may contain a verification code or link, and interact with the browser tab where you are signing in. Below is a complete breakdown of every data request, why it is needed, and what we do not collect.

1. Google account email address

OAuth scope: https://www.googleapis.com/auth/userinfo.email

Why we request it: You sign in with Google to connect Gmail. We need your email address to confirm sign-in and show which account is connected in the popup.

What we read: Your Google account email address only.

What we do with it: Display it in the extension popup. Stored locally while signed in.

What we do not do: Sell it, share with advertisers, or send it to a VerifyFill server.

2. Gmail inbox messages (read-only)

OAuth scope: https://www.googleapis.com/auth/gmail.readonly

Why we request it: Websites send verification codes and links to Gmail. Without read access, VerifyFill cannot detect them. Read-only means we cannot send, delete, or modify email.

What we read:

  • Inbox messages only — not sent, drafts, spam, or trash
  • Received in the last 15 minutes
  • Up to 20 most recent messages in that window
  • Subject line, body text, sender, and message ID per message

When we read Gmail: After sign-in; every ~1 minute while active; on Check now; when you focus a verification input or land on a check-your-email page.

What we do not do: Upload email to a server, read your full mailbox, use data for ads, or access Gmail when signed out.

3. The website page you are using

Not a Google OAuth scope — Chrome extension tab permission.

Why we need it: To show a fill prompt near your input and autofill the code on the page you are using.

What we do not do: Track browsing history, collect pages for advertising, or send page content to a server.

4. Local storage on your device

Sign-in state, connected email, OAuth tokens (via Chrome), and IDs of used messages/codes/links. Not a copy of your inbox.

5. Data we do not request

Google Drive, Calendar, Contacts, Photos, send-mail access, delete-mail access, or your Google password.

6. Third parties

VerifyFill communicates only with Google services (Sign-In, Gmail API). See Google's Privacy Policy.

7. Your control

Sign out in the extension, revoke access in Google Account permissions, or uninstall the extension at any time.
