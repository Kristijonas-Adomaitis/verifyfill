VerifyFill Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 26, 2026 · VerifyFill

VerifyFill is a Chrome extension that reads verification codes and email verification links from your Gmail inbox and offers to autofill them on websites you visit. This policy explains what data the extension accesses, how it is used, and what is stored.

Summary

  • VerifyFill only requests read-only access to Gmail.
  • Email content is processed on your device inside the extension.
  • We do not operate servers that receive your email or verification codes.
  • You can disconnect access at any time by signing out in the extension or revoking access in your Google Account.

Data we access

When you sign in with Google, VerifyFill may access:

  • Your Google account email address (to show which account is connected)
  • Recent Gmail messages in your inbox, limited to messages that may contain verification codes or verification links
  • The page you are on when you focus a verification input or when the extension shows a fill prompt

VerifyFill uses the Google Gmail API with the https://www.googleapis.com/auth/gmail.readonly scope. The extension cannot send, delete, or modify your email.

How we use data

Data is used only to provide the extension’s core features:

  • Detect verification codes and verification links in recent email
  • Display a fill prompt near the relevant input on the current tab
  • Autofill a code or open a verification link when you choose to do so
  • Remember which codes, links, and messages you have already used so prompts are not shown again

VerifyFill does not use your data for advertising, profiling, or resale.

What is stored locally

On your device, VerifyFill may store:

  • Whether you are signed in and your connected Gmail address
  • OAuth tokens managed by Chrome’s identity system
  • Identifiers for emails and verification items you have already used (to avoid repeat prompts)
  • The most recently detected verification item until it is used or dismissed

This information is stored in Chrome’s local extension storage on your computer. It is not uploaded to a VerifyFill backend.

What we do not collect

  • We do not run our own servers that receive your inbox contents
  • We do not sell or share your personal data with third parties for marketing
  • We do not track your browsing history outside what is needed to show fill prompts on the active tab

Third-party services

VerifyFill communicates only with Google services required for sign-in and Gmail access:

  • Google OAuth / Google Account sign-in
  • Gmail API

Google’s use of data is governed by Google’s Privacy Policy.

Permissions

The extension requests Chrome permissions needed for its functionality, including:

  • identity — sign in with Google
  • storage — save sign-in state and used verification items locally
  • alarms — check Gmail periodically while signed in
  • tabs / activeTab / scripting — show fill prompts and autofill on the page you are using
  • host permissions — access Google APIs and run on websites where you want autofill

Data retention

Local extension data remains on your device until you sign out, uninstall the extension, or clear extension data in Chrome. Signing out removes stored verification history from the extension.

Your choices

  • Sign out from the extension popup at any time
  • Revoke VerifyFill’s access in your Google Account permissions
  • Uninstall the extension to remove it from your browser

Children

VerifyFill is not directed at children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children.

Changes to this policy

We may update this policy from time to time. The “Last updated” date at the top of this page will reflect the latest version.
