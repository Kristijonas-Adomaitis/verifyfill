VerifyFill

Chrome Extension

VerifyFill is developed and owned by Kristijonas.

Purpose

VerifyFill exists so you can finish signing in to websites without constantly switching between Gmail and the site you are on. When a service sends a verification code or a confirmation link to your email, this application finds it and helps you use it on the page where you are trying to log in or create an account.

How it works

VerifyFill is a Chrome extension. After you install it and sign in with Google:

  • You open a website that asks for a verification code or tells you to check your email.
  • VerifyFill searches your inbox using the Gmail access described below.
  • If a matching code or link is found, it shows a prompt on the page you are using.
  • You choose to fill the code into the form or open the confirmation link.

Why VerifyFill requests your data — full transparency

VerifyFill only works if it can (1) know which Google account you connected, (2) read recent Gmail messages that may contain a verification code or link, and (3) interact with the browser tab where you are trying to sign in. This section explains every type of user data the extension requests, the exact purpose of each request, what is read, what is stored, what is not collected, and what you can do to stop access.

1. Google account email address

OAuth scope: https://www.googleapis.com/auth/userinfo.email

Why we request it: You sign in with Google to connect your Gmail account to the extension. We need your Google account email address so the extension can confirm sign-in succeeded and show you which account is currently connected in the extension popup.

What we read: Your Google account email address only (for example, you@example.com).

What we do with it: Display it in the extension popup so you know which Gmail account VerifyFill is using. It is stored locally on your device while you remain signed in.

What we do not do with it: We do not sell it, share it with advertisers, use it for marketing, or send it to a VerifyFill server. We do not use it to access any Google product other than signing you in and labeling the connected account.

2. Gmail inbox messages (read-only)

OAuth scope: https://www.googleapis.com/auth/gmail.readonly

Why we request it: Websites send verification codes and email confirmation links to Gmail. Without read access to your inbox, VerifyFill cannot detect those codes or links and cannot offer to autofill them. This is the core purpose of the application. Read-only access means the extension can read mail but cannot send, delete, label, or modify any message.

What we read: Only when you are signed in, VerifyFill queries your Gmail inbox for messages that match all of the following limits:

  • Messages in your inbox only (not sent mail, drafts, spam, or trash)
  • Messages received in the last 15 minutes
  • Up to the 20 most recent messages matching that time window
  • For each message: the subject line, body text (plain text or HTML converted to text), sender, and message ID

How we use what we read: The extension scans subject and body text only to detect patterns that look like verification codes (for example, 6-digit OTP codes) or email confirmation / sign-in links. If a likely match is found, VerifyFill shows a prompt on the website tab you are using so you can fill the code or open the link. Message content is processed in your browser on your device.

When we read Gmail:

  • After you sign in
  • Approximately every 1 minute while the extension is signed in and active
  • When you click Check now in the extension popup
  • When you focus a verification input on a website or land on a “check your email” page (the extension begins watching for a matching message)

What we store locally from Gmail:

  • The most recently detected verification code or link (until you use it or dismiss the prompt)
  • IDs of Gmail messages, codes, and links you have already used (so VerifyFill does not prompt you again for the same item)

What we do not do with Gmail data:

  • We do not upload your email content to a VerifyFill server — there is no VerifyFill backend that receives mail
  • We do not read your entire mailbox — only the limited inbox window described above
  • We do not use Gmail data for advertising, profiling, analytics, or resale
  • We do not send, delete, move, or edit any email
  • We do not access Gmail when you are signed out

3. The website page you are using (browser tab)

Not a Google OAuth scope. This is access through normal Chrome extension permissions on the active browser tab.

Why we need it: VerifyFill must know when you are on a page that is asking for a verification code or telling you to check your email, so it can show a fill prompt near the correct input and autofill the code if you choose. Without access to the page you are on, the extension could read Gmail but could not help you on the website.

What we access on the page:

  • Whether you have focused an input field that looks like a verification code field
  • The page content needed to show a small fill prompt and insert a code into the focused field, or open a verification link when you click the prompt

What we do not do on web pages: We do not track your general browsing history, do not collect page content for advertising, and do not send page content to a VerifyFill server. Page interaction happens locally in the extension.

4. Local storage on your device

Why we store data locally: So you stay signed in between browser sessions and so VerifyFill does not repeatedly prompt you for codes or links you already used.

What may be stored in Chrome extension storage on your computer:

  • Whether you are signed in
  • Your connected Google account email address
  • OAuth tokens managed by Chrome’s identity system (not by a VerifyFill server)
  • IDs of Gmail messages, verification codes, and links you have already used

What we do not store: A copy of your full inbox. Only the limited items above.

5. Data we do not request at all

To be explicit, VerifyFill does not request access to:

  • Google Drive, Calendar, Contacts, Photos, or any other Google service
  • Gmail permissions beyond read-only inbox access
  • Your Google password (sign-in is handled by Google’s own sign-in screen)
  • Permission to send email on your behalf
  • Permission to delete or modify email

6. Third parties

VerifyFill communicates only with Google’s own services (Google Sign-In, Gmail API, and Google Account token management through Chrome). We do not send your data to any other company. Google’s handling of data under those services is covered by Google’s Privacy Policy.

7. Your control

You are never required to use VerifyFill’s autofill prompt — you can always type a code or open a link yourself. You can stop all data access by:

  • Clicking Sign out in the extension popup
  • Revoking VerifyFill in your Google Account permissions
  • Uninstalling the extension from Chrome

Signing out removes locally stored verification history from the extension.
